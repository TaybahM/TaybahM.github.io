My Journey

I was born in Virginia and I live in Dallas, Texas.

My father taught me a lot about design and the way mechanics work. He would teach me logical approaches to difficult physical problems. He is the reason I was inclined to learn more about design and software.

Curiosity is what brought me to where I am today. When I don't get how some technology works, I learn it in depth to understand the mechanics of the structure. I've studied 3-D design in an effort to further advance in my field. I quickly realized that my passion happened to be a combination of software and mechanical Engineering. I am currently pursuing a degree in Mechanical Engineering.

When I first started pushing my design interest, I started selling small framework of Arabic calligraphy. Many people requested specific designs which I would create on an old vector art program. I would then take the vector deisng, print it, and use it as a reference to do calligraphy on scratch paper.

How did I get into Digital Art?

I soon realized that in order to progress in this calligraphy I needed to take more classes and learn to use better editing software. This is where I started my digital art journey. I began creating similar designs as vector art and selling them to clients who would use them for their own personal purposes. After some time I began experimenting with different Arabic scripts and also taught classes on Arabic composition and writing structure.

Next Steps

As I learned more about design with Arabic, I decided to explore with print material. This pushed me to create designs for clients who would print them on stationary items. I had one major client in Maryland who would sell her items with my designs at different vendors.

Visual Identity

Some time after my calligraphy journey, I began to be fascinated by logos and visual/brand identity. To further my skills I posted ads to create logos for small companies around the area and got some pretty good offers. I created a logo for the same company that I sold print designs to, to a Muslim female basketball player and for a few mosques in DC. These were a few of the first freelance gigs that pushed me into visual/brand identity.

After exploring logo design, I pushed myself to work harder on product design and marketing. I then took social media marketing courses and landed a job at a Coding company based in Ashburn, Virginia where I taught kids the basics of HTML and JavaScript. I also became the lead designer and social media marketer.

Brand Identity

My most recent design obsession has been brand design. I have been taking classes to learn brand identity and learn more about color theory.
