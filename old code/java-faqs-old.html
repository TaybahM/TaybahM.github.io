Frequently Asked Questions

Read on for more information about what I offer, what to expect and how to contact me. If you're a designer and have questions check Designer FAQS. Can't find the asnwers to your questions? Send me an email after checking out this page.

If you would like to support my work, send me an email and I'll get back to you with the next steps.

Please keep in mind before emailing me, that I do not sell website templates or create websites. I can work with your web designer to provide content for your website and I may assist with the coding process, however I will not provide a website from scratch for your company. I may create brand design and templates for your web designer to follow and use as a reference.

Client FAQS

What can I expect when I shoot an email?

If the email is asking me to do something completely out of my expertise, you'll get an image of a confused cat in response. However, if it's an important question, I will provide my best response as soon as possible. (I can send the confused cat too if you'd like at the end)

What are your rates?

My rates depend on the intensity of the project and the time I am given to complete it. If you would like an estimate, you can send me an email with what you're looking for.

How long do I have to wait for a response?

If I am not enjoying a Taco Bell burrito while watching an episode of The Office, my email responses will be pretty quick, usually within 24 hours. I update my website constantly and if I am out of town, that will be shown on the site.

Where can I see your work?

Majority of the projects that I have worked on are displayed on the homepage. If you would like to see some of the older, more embarassing work that I've done when I first started graphic design, theres a non-refundable fee of $15 to view them.

Can you throw a quick logo together for me?

Unfortunately, I have gotten this question many times in the last few years. I had to learn the hard way that there is no such thing as a quick logo, so no I cannot.

What is the News Wall?

The News Wall is a place where I post the latest design trends or design updates. I usually update them once a week.

What are the client requirements?

  1. 1. Be at least 18 years old
  2. 2. Have access to a private computer with a secure, reliable internet connection
  3. 3. Be located in the United States
  4. 4. Must be fluent in English and cable of clearly communicating with Volunteers

Designer FAQS

Where did you get your website template?

I got it from HTML5 UP. This website has fully responsive, free and customizable websites that are built on HTML5 and CSS3. I forked a repository to my github account and turned it into a github page. Then I cloned the repo on VS Code and customized it.

Where can I find design resources, tricks or inspiration?

  • My resources page.
  • I recommend taking a look at Will Patterson's youtube channel. His page is filled with great graphic design tips and tricks.
  • Pinterest is a great place to look through for design inspiration, it's one of my favorite sites to take a look at when I'm stuck on a design.

What is the time commitment?

The time commitment is flexible and based on the volunteerâ€™s schedule. A minumum 1 hour per week is required.

Can I volunteer for school credit?

Yes, as long as the school is aware that Anchoheart is not an official government nonprofit.

Are volunteers supervised?

At first volunteers are monitored closely and checked on. Later on they are able to work independantly. Check in as needed and attend individual and group meetings or support hang outs.

How are volunteers trained??

Volunteers have orientation and training by phone one on one with the owner and founder at a time that is convenient for them.

What policies must volunteers follow?

Volunteers must sign and follow the volunteer form, a non disclosure agreement and the guidance given during the orientation and training
